## Messaging Principles

1. **Lead with pain or outcome, not product features.** The first sentence should make them nod, not learn.
2. **Do not over-explain.** First touch is about getting a reply, not closing a deal.
3. **Mention the sample memo early.** It is the strongest proof asset. Let the work speak.
4. **Be honest about stage.** "Design partner pilot" is more credible than pretending to be established.
5. **No AI hype.** Do not say "AI-powered" in the subject line. Finance buyers are skeptical of AI claims. Lead with the outcome (board-ready memo, audit trail, 48 hours).
6. **Personalize when possible.** Reference their recent raise, new hire, accounting system, or a specific pain signal. Generic messages get ignored.
7. **Keep it short.** Under 150 words for email. Under 100 for LinkedIn DM. Under 75 for follow-ups.
