# ICP Shortlist Framework v1

**Purpose:** Define who E-Solutions should pursue first for design partner conversations, and how to identify them.

---

## Ideal Early Customer Profile

The best first customers share these characteristics:

| Dimension | Ideal | Acceptable |
|-----------|-------|------------|
| Stage | Series A, Series B | Seed (with board), Late Seed |
| Employees | 30–150 | 20–250 |
| ARR | $3M–$20M | $1M–$30M |
| Industry | B2B SaaS | B2B services, fintech, vertical SaaS |
| Finance team | 1–2 people (Controller + maybe FP&A) | Solo fractional CFO, 3-person team |
| Accounting system | QuickBooks Online, Xero | NetSuite (adds COA complexity) |
| Board reporting | Monthly variance memo or board deck with financials | Quarterly investor updates |
| Geography | US | US, Canada, UK (English-speaking) |

---

## Strong-Fit Indicators

These are signals that a company is worth founder outreach right now:

1. **Recently raised.** Series A or B closed in the last 6–12 months. Board reporting expectations just increased. New investors want more rigorous financials.
2. **Hired first Controller or FP&A person.** This person inherited a spreadsheet mess and is building process from scratch. They are the user and often the internal champion.
3. **Board meeting cadence is monthly.** Monthly reporting = monthly pain = monthly value from E-Solutions.
4. **Finance team is clearly under-resourced.** Signals: founder still involved in close, Controller doing bookkeeping and FP&A, late board decks, inconsistent reporting format.
5. **Uses QBO or Xero.** Clean CSV/Excel export path. No heavy ERP integration needed.
6. **SaaS business model.** Standard revenue categories (subscription, services, usage) map cleanly to our COA template. Predictable variance patterns.
7. **Founder is accessible.** If you can reach the founder or CFO directly (warm intro, shared network, community overlap), the sales cycle compresses dramatically.

---

## Disqualifiers

Do not pursue these companies. They will waste founder time.

1. **Pre-revenue or pre-product.** No GL data to analyze. No board reporting obligation.
2. **Enterprise (500+ employees, $50M+ ARR).** Long sales cycle, procurement process, enterprise security requirements we cannot meet yet.
3. **No board or investor reporting obligation.** If they do not produce variance memos or board financials, there is no pain to solve.
4. **Finance team > 5 people.** They likely have established tools and processes. Switching cost is too high for a pilot.
5. **Heavy regulatory environment.** Healthcare, banking, government — compliance requirements exceed what we can offer at pilot stage.
6. **Non-English accounting systems.** Our COA mapping and narrative generation are English-only.
7. **Founder/CFO is unreachable.** If the only path is through a gatekeeper or generic contact form, deprioritize. Founder-led outreach requires founder-to-founder access.

---

## Weak-Fit Indicators

Not disqualifiers, but lower priority. Put at the bottom of the list.

1. **Seed stage with no institutional board.** May not produce formal variance memos yet.
2. **NetSuite user.** COA exports are more complex, mapping takes longer, potential for messy multi-entity data.
3. **Professional services firm (non-SaaS).** Revenue recognition can be complex. Variance patterns are less standard.
4. **Fractional CFO as primary finance person.** May have limited bandwidth for pilot feedback. Decision authority may be with the founder.
5. **Company in cost-cutting mode.** $1,500/month may face budget scrutiny even if the pain is real.

## Urgency Triggers

These events create time-sensitive windows where the pain is acutely felt:

| Trigger | Why It Matters |
|---------|----------------|
| Just raised Series A/B | Board expectations increased. First "real" board reporting cycle. |
| First Controller hire in last 3 months | Building process from scratch. Open to new tools. |
| CFO/VP Finance departure | Finance function temporarily under-resourced. Someone is scrambling. |
| Board meeting in 2–3 weeks | Immediate pain. "How are we going to get this done?" |
| Missed or late board deck last quarter | Embarrassment creates motivation. |
| Auditor flagged reporting gaps | External pressure to improve process. |
| Preparing for next fundraise | Investors will scrutinize financial reporting quality. |

---

## Likely Pain Signals

What prospects might say or post that indicates the problem:

- "We spend the first week of every month closing and building the board deck"
- "Our board wants monthly financials but we only have bandwidth for quarterly"
- "I need someone to own the monthly variance analysis"
- "We're looking for a fractional CFO to help with board reporting"
- "Our close process takes too long"
- "We need better financial reporting for our board"
- Job postings for Controller or FP&A with "board reporting" in the description

---

## Likely Objections

| Objection | What It Really Means |
|-----------|---------------------|
| "We already have a process" | It works but it is painful. Probe on time and auditability. |
| "I don't trust AI with board numbers" | They need to hear about human review and audit trail. |
| "We use Mosaic/Jirav/Cube" | Those are dashboards, not memo production. Different problem. |
| "$1,500/month is expensive for a startup" | Frame as: what does your controller's time cost? |
| "We'd need to see it work on our data first" | Good sign. Offer the free test run. |
| "This seems early-stage" | Acknowledge it. 90 days, no lock-in, significant discount. |
| "Can you integrate with our accounting system?" | Not yet — but manual upload keeps onboarding fast. |

---

## What Makes a Prospect Worth Personal Founder Outreach

A prospect is worth direct founder time if they score 4+ on these 6 criteria:

1. **ICP fit:** Stage, size, finance team, and accounting system match
2. **Reachable buyer:** You can get to the CFO or founder within 1–2 degrees
3. **Active pain signal:** Recent raise, new hire, job posting, or public comment about reporting
4. **Board reporting obligation:** Monthly or quarterly board meetings with financial review
5. **Design partner potential:** Willing to give feedback, accessible for weekly calls, reasonable expectations
6. **Reference potential:** Would make a credible case study (recognizable brand, strong network, willing to be public)

If a prospect hits 4+ of these, they belong in the top 10. Spend time crafting a custom message. If they hit 2–3, they go in the pipeline but get a templated first touch.
