# Convergence Plan

## Phase 1: Prove the wedge

**Objective:** Validate that a repeatable monthly variance memo workflow delivers measurable value to lean finance teams.

**Key outputs:**
- Working variance memo workflow (manual + AI-assisted, not fully automated)
- Sample deliverable produced from realistic or anonymized data
- Demo narrative that communicates the value in under 5 minutes
- Defined pilot package with clear scope, timeline, and expectations

**Risks:**
- Variance memos may be too heterogeneous across companies to standardize
- Data access and formatting may create onboarding friction that kills pilots early
- AI-generated narrative quality may not meet CFO expectations without heavy editing

**Exit criteria:**
- At least 1 complete sample variance memo deliverable exists
- Demo has been shown to at least 5 ICP-fit prospects
- At least 2 prospects agree to a design partnership

---

## Phase 2: Win design partners

**Objective:** Onboard 2–3 design partners running live monthly variance memo workflows on real data.

**Key outputs:**
- Signed design partner agreements (lightweight, non-binding or minimal commitment)
- Onboarding flow documented and tested
- First live monthly memo produced for each partner
- Feedback collected and logged after each cycle
- Iteration backlog based on real usage

**Risks:**
- Design partners may churn after one cycle if value is unclear
- Data integration issues may slow onboarding beyond patience thresholds
- Scope creep from partner requests may pull focus away from the wedge

**Exit criteria:**
- At least 2 design partners have completed 2+ monthly cycles
- Documented time savings and qualitative feedback from each partner
- Clear signal on willingness to pay (verbal or written)
- Onboarding process is repeatable without heroic effort

---

## Phase 3: Convert to paid and tighten repeatability

**Objective:** Convert design partners to paid pilots and document a repeatable sales and delivery process.

**Key outputs:**
- Pricing model defined and tested
- At least 1 paid pilot signed
- Repeatable onboarding and delivery playbook
- Sales motion documented (outreach → demo → pilot → paid)
- Product roadmap informed by real usage data

**Risks:**
- Partners may resist paying if they perceive the service as experimental
- Pricing may be too high for early-stage companies or too low to sustain the business
- Delivery quality may degrade as the number of clients increases without automation

**Exit criteria:**
- At least 1 paying customer
- Documented and tested sales + delivery process
- Unit economics estimated (cost to deliver vs. revenue per client)
- Clear decision on whether to invest in product automation vs. managed service scaling
